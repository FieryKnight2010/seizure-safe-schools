# Quiz: Understanding Epilepsy

Test yourself. Click each question to reveal the answer.

??? quiz "1. About how common is epilepsy?"
    About **1 in 100 people**. Most schools have students with epilepsy.

??? quiz "2. True or false: every seizure involves convulsions (shaking)."
    **False.** Some seizures look like brief staring, confusion, or repeated movements.
    Absence and many focal seizures have no convulsions.

??? quiz "3. What is the difference between a seizure and epilepsy?"
    A **seizure** is a single event of abnormal electrical activity in the brain.
    **Epilepsy** is the condition of having a tendency toward repeated, unprovoked seizures.

??? quiz "4. Name two common seizure triggers."
    Any two of: missed medication, lack of sleep, illness or fever, stress, flashing lights.

??? quiz "5. What is the 'postictal' phase?"
    The recovery period right after a seizure, when a person may be tired, confused, or
    emotional. Staff should stay with the student and reassure them.

??? quiz "6. What is rescue medication?"
    A medication prescribed for a seizure that lasts too long or repeats, given according to
    the student's healthcare-provider-written seizure action plan.
